How it works. The speed trace drives a longitudinal vehicle model (rolling + aero + inertia, drivetrain losses, regen) to get the pack's power demand. An R-int equivalent-circuit model turns that into current, voltage and SOC. The two SOH figures are then estimated blind from those logs: capacity by ampere-hour counting between two rest-OCV points over a multi-cycle test, resistance by regressing overpotential against current (η = R·I + c). Drag Battery Condition and watch the estimates track the true state. Model is illustrative — a representative WLTP-class trace, not the exact regulatory cycle.

How it works. Heat comes from two sources: irreversible Joule heating (I²R, which grows as the pack ages and resistance rises) and reversible entropic heat (I·T·dU/dT). A lumped-capacitance balance C·dT/dt = Qgen − UA·(T−Tcool) integrates pack temperature over eight back-to-back cycles; steady-state temperature is Tcool + Qavg/UA. Push Resistance growth up (Battery Ageing group, shared with the SOH view) and watch an aged pack run hotter.
